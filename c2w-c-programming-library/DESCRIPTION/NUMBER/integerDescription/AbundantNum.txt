	
	
	
	SELECTED:
	
		Abundant Number 

	DESCRIPTION: 
	
		In number theory, an abundant number or excessive number is a 
		number for which the sum of its proper divisors is greater than 
	        the number.
	             
	EXAMPLE:
	 
		The integer 12 is the first abundant number. Its 
		proper divisors are 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 for a total of 16.
